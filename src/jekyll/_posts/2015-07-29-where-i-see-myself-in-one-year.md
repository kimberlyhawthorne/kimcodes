I wouldn't be a journalism school graduate if I didn't cite my sources. Thus in the spirit of ethical journalism, I hereby proclaim that I stole this idea&mdash;many, many months ago&mdash;from <a href='http://jenniferdewalt.com/' target='_blank'>Jennifer Dewalt</a>, who coded 180 websites in 180 days.

In an effort to eat a less varied diet, alienate my friends and, above all, sharpen my development skills, I too will be undertaking a programming challenge: to code 52 projects in 52 weeks. Think of it as a nerdier version of Julie & Julia, with much more typing and much fewer calories.

My original intention was for this blog and <a href='/'>newly recoded portfolio</a> to be my first project. But, seeing as I coded the bulk of both sites more than three months ago, I've forgotten any insights or things I screwed up. So, you'll just have to live with the fact that this blog and accompanying portfolio are coded with <a href='http://jekyllrb.com' target='_blank'>Jekyll</a>. Three cheers for templating! 

The eternity elapsed between creating the Git repository for this site and actually launching it made me realize that sticking to a rigid one-project-per-week schedule may not be realistic. Because sometimes you have to <a href='http://www.dalmac.org/' target='_blank'>ride your bike across the state of Michigan</a>, or help your sister move to <a href='http://mimsydoodle.tumblr.com/' target='_blank'>San Francisco</a>, or go to New York to visit a friend and almost lose your eye when you get a speck of dust under your contact lens in a dirty karaoke bar.

In light of this, I've settled on a few guidelines:

### The Guidelines

* I will TRY to code one project per week. Some weeks may have none and others two, but I will have 52 completed projects in the end. 
* I will also TRY to code all projects using plain ol' Javascript, rather than jQuery or other libraries.
* I will more than likely use a CSS preprocessor for all of my projects because who wouldn't?
* I will ensure modern browser compatibility for all projects. That does not include you, Internet Explorer 7.
* I will add all projects to <a href='http://github.com/kforkimber/kimcodes'>this Github repository</a>.
* I will, whenever possible, code with my cat on my lap.

And so, as my grandmother would say, "we're off like a herd of turtles!"
